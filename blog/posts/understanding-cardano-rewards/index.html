Understanding the math behind Cardano staking rewards.

June 21, 2021

Foreword

The goal of this post is to inform clients of Stake.Green staking service or of any other staking service on how staking rewards are calculated and distributed on the Cardano (ADA) blockchain.

First of all, it is worth mentioning that reward computation and distribution is fully automatised by the Cardano blockchain. It means that by delegating your ADA to a pool you are 100% assured to receive you share of the mining rewards that the pool is entitled to.

However, maximising the amount of rewards that the pool is entitled to is subject to state-of-the-art pool management and monitoring. At Stake.Green we make our mission to provide this high standard of mining pool management in order to deliver the best possible APY to our clients.

In the rest of this article you will find complete and transparent information on Cardano rewards and on how the quality of pool management affects those rewards (follow the ⚠️).

If you are mainly interested by the actual reward that you will get by delegating to Stake.Green feel free to directly jump to this section.

Total amount of reward

At the end of each epoch (currently 5 days) Cardano allocates a total reward amount RtotR_\text{tot}Rtot​ of ADA that will be split between all mining pools and treasury. The value of this total reward is computed as follows:

Rtot=min(η,1)⋅ρ⋅reserve+feesR_\text{tot} = \text{min}(\eta,1) \cdot \rho \cdot \text{reserve} + \text{fees}Rtot​=min(η,1)⋅ρ⋅reserve+fees

Where:

  • reserve\text{reserve}reserve: the amount of ADA that have not been issued yet. The Cardano blockhain sets a maximum attainable supply of 45 billion ADA. Hence, at any time the following equation holds: reserve+total ADA supply=maximum ADA supply\text{reserve}+\text{total ADA supply} = \text{maximum ADA supply}reserve+total ADA supply=maximum ADA supply. The current value of the ADA reserve is given on our ADA Live Data page.

  • ρ\rhoρ: a Protocol Parameter indicating how much of the reserve should be allocated to rewards at each epoch. Hence, due to reward distribution, the ADA reserve\text{reserve}reserve diminishes with time and the total ADA supply increases.

  • η\etaη: the ratio between the number of blocks that were actually minted in the epoch over the ideal number of blocks that should have been minted during the epoch. The ideal number of blocks that should have been minted during the epoch is given by the following formula: activeSlotsCoeff×epochLength\text{activeSlotsCoeff}\times\text{epochLength}activeSlotsCoeff×epochLength where activeSlotsCoeff\text{activeSlotsCoeff}activeSlotsCoeff and epochLength\text{epochLength}epochLength are Protocol Parameters. The current ideal number of blocks per epoch is 2160021 60021600 which represents one block every 20s.

  • fees\text{fees}fees: the sum of all the transaction fees of the epoch. ADA imposes a minimum transaction fee per transaction of: minFeeA×size(tx)+minFeeB\text{minFeeA}\times\text{size(tx)}+\text{minFeeB}minFeeA×size(tx)+minFeeB with size(tx)\text{size(tx)}size(tx) the size of the transaction in bytes where minFeeA\text{minFeeA}minFeeA and minFeeB\text{minFeeB}minFeeB are Protocol Parameters.

Treasury tax

The first thing to come off the total reward is the treasury tax. A fraction τ\tauτ of RtotR_{\text{tot}}Rtot​ is directly distributed to the Treasury whose goal is to:

Provision funds to develop Cardano activities through a voting process.

Hence, you can think of it as a traditional state tax. Fraction τ\tauτ is a Protocol Parameter.

After the treasury tax, the epoch’s reward amount RRR which remains for mining pools is:

R=(min(η,1)⋅ρ⋅reserve+fees)⋅(1−τ)R = (\text{min}(\eta,1) \cdot \rho \cdot \text{reserve} + \text{fees}) \cdot (1-\tau)R=(min(η,1)⋅ρ⋅reserve+fees)⋅(1−τ)

The fraction of RRR that each mining pool will receive depends on two factors: the stake pledged my the managing entity of the pool and the total stake delegated stake to the pool (including the pledge).

Stake pledged. The pool’s pledge is an amount of ADA that is owned and pledged by the pool managing entity. Stake Green’s current pledge can be found on our ADA Pool Specification page.

⚠️ If a pool does not provide proof of its declared pledge rewards for the epoch will automatically be set to 000. Hence the pool’s clients wont receive any reward for that epoch.

Stake delegated. The pool’s total amount of delegated ADA (including the pledge). This total is the sum of all the ADA that the pool’s clients delegate to the pool plus the pool’s pledge. Stake Green’s current delegated stake can be found on our ADA Pool Specification page.

Ignoring pledge influence

Most of the complexity of the above formula for f(s,σ)f(s,\sigma)f(s,σ) comes from the fact that the Cardano blockchain wishes to give some importance to the pledge that a mining pool is providing during the mining process. Having a high pledge gives more credit to a mining pool entity who is willing to temporarily freeze that pledge before the epoch to ensure optimal mining.

Let say for now that we ignore the pledge influence factor and set a0=0a_0 = 0a0​=0. Then the formula of f(s,σ)f(s,\sigma)f(s,σ) simply becomes:

Pool reward in practice

The formula of f(s,σ)f(s,\sigma)f(s,σ) gives the theoretical maximum amount of reward that the pool is entitled to for the epoch. However, in practice, this amount is ponderated by the pool performance factor.

Pool performance factor. The probability for a mining pool to be chosen to mint a block is σ~\tilde{\sigma}σ~ where σ~\tilde{\sigma}σ~ is the delegated staking of the pool relative to the total of staked ADA. At the date of this article, approximatively 70% of the total ADA supply was delegated.

However, the realised fraction of block minted during the period is β=Number of blocks minted by the poolNumber of blocks minted during the epoch\beta = \frac{\text{Number of blocks minted by the pool}}{\text{Number of blocks minted during the epoch}}β=Number of blocks minted during the epochNumber of blocks minted by the pool​.

The pool’s perfomance factor is the ratio β/σ~\beta/\tilde{\sigma}β/σ~ which gives a metric of how ideally the pool performed during the epoch.

Due to the randomness of the pool selection process, the ratio β/σ~\beta/\tilde{\sigma}β/σ~ will fluctuate from epochs to epochs: sometimes it will be greater than 1 as the pool was lucky and minted more blocks than expected, and sometimes it will be lower than 111, the pool was unlucky and minted less blocks than expected. But, in average, the ratio β/σ~\beta/\tilde{\sigma}β/σ~ should be equal to 1 (in practice, extremely close to 1).

⚠️ If a pool does not operate correctly and has downtime during a mining period, the performance indicator β/σ~\beta/\tilde{\sigma}β/σ~ will be biased negatively and will be lower than 1 in average.
